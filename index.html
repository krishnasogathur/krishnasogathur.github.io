About

Building solvers at the quantum–classical boundary

My work sits at the intersection of quantum optics and numerical methods. I'm particularly interested in what happens to a quantum system under continuous observation — a question with direct relevance to neutral-atom quantum computing, where atoms need to survive many imaging cycles without being lost from the trap.

On the simulation side, I build solvers that go beyond standard toolkits: a grid-based MCWF approach for anharmonic potentials like optical lattices, and a variational Gaussian TDVP method that scales to many modes without the exponential cost of exact diagonalisation.
