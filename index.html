About

I work at the intersection of quantum optics, atomic physics, and numerical methods — particularly on understanding what happens when you trap atoms and continuously image them, where decoherence, photon recoil, and non-Hermitian dynamics all interplay.

Currently building solvers for open quantum systems at scales where exact methods fail: a grid-based MCWF approach for soft anharmonic potentials, and a variational Gaussian TDVP solver that extends naturally to many modes.
