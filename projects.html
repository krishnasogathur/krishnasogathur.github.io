2025 – 2026 Python MCWF Quantum optics

Grid-based MCWF solver for soft potentials

Open-source · soft-mcwf

Standard quantum optics toolkits represent motional states in the Fock basis — natural for harmonic traps, but poorly suited to anharmonic potentials like Gaussian tweezers or optical lattices where many Fock states are needed for convergence. This solver works directly on a real-space momentum grid, representing the wavefunction as ψ(p) with position obtained via FFT, giving an exact description for arbitrary soft potentials.

Applied to a Yb-171 atom in a 1D optical lattice (759 nm) under continuous near-resonant imaging (399 nm): photon recoil drives heating and eventual loss. Observables include Wigner function evolution, Wannier site population dynamics, and off-diagonal density matrix coherences. Trajectories are parallelised via joblib with incremental disk saves.

2025 – 2026 Python TDVP Variational methods

Variational Gaussian TDVP for multimode open systems

Open-source · multimode-tdvp

Exact simulation of open quantum systems scales exponentially with mode number — a density matrix for d modes with Fock cutoff N has Nd entries. This solver represents the state as a superposition of displaced, squeezed Gaussian coherent states, with variational parameters evolved via the McLachlan TDVP principle. The problem reduces to one scaling as O(NGauss × d), enabling multimode simulations out of reach for exact methods.

Lindblad dissipation is handled via quantum trajectories: between jumps, the non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian drives TDVP evolution; at a jump event, the operator is applied analytically within the Gaussian manifold (spin decay, cavity loss, recoil displacement). Benchmarked against QuTiP on a Kerr anharmonic oscillator, matching the exact phase-space distribution with only 4 Gaussian components.

Master’s project: Neutral atoms in optical tweezers under resonant imaging

Master’s thesis · IIT Madras

Studied the quantum dynamics of a neutral atom trapped in an optical tweezer under continuous resonant imaging. Imaging photons scatter at rate Γ, imparting ±ℏk recoil kicks and driving transitions between internal states — causing heating and eventual loss from the trap. This is a fundamental constraint for tweezer-based quantum computing and quantum sensing, where high-fidelity mid-circuit measurements require surviving many photon scatters.

The simulation tracks the full motional + internal state quantum trajectory, enabling direct comparison against classical trajectory simulations and characterisation of the quantum-to-classical transition in the heating dynamics.
