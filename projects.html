2025 – 2026 Python MCWF Quantum optics

Grid-based MCWF solver for soft potentials

Open-source · soft-mcwf

Standard quantum optics toolkits represent motional states in the Fock basis — natural for harmonic traps, but poorly convergent for anharmonic potentials like Gaussian tweezers or optical lattices. This solver works directly on a real-space momentum grid, representing the wavefunction as ψ(p) with position obtained via FFT, giving an exact description for arbitrary soft potentials with no basis truncation.

Applied to a Yb-171 atom in a 1D optical lattice (759 nm) under continuous near-resonant imaging (399 nm): photon recoil drives heating and loss. Observables include the full Wigner function, Wannier site population dynamics, and off-diagonal density matrix coherences. Trajectories are parallelised via joblib.

2025 – 2026 Python TDVP Variational methods

Variational Gaussian TDVP for multimode open systems

Open-source · multimode-tdvp

Exact simulation of open quantum systems scales exponentially with mode number. This solver represents the state as a superposition of displaced, squeezed Gaussian coherent states, with variational parameters evolved under the McLachlan TDVP principle. The problem reduces from an exponential Hilbert space to one scaling as O(NGauss × d), making multimode problems tractable.

Lindblad dissipation is handled via quantum trajectories: between jumps the non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian drives TDVP evolution; at a jump event the operator is applied analytically within the Gaussian manifold. Benchmarked against QuTiP on a Kerr anharmonic oscillator, matching the exact result with only 4 Gaussian components.
